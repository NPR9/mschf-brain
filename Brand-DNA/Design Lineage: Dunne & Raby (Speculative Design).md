## Design Lineage: Dunne & Raby (Speculative Design)

MSCHF explicitly cites **Anthony Dunne and Fiona Raby** as a direct design influence — specifically in the context of Jesus Shoes (Archive #7, 2019):

> "Speculative design principles assert that design is also useful — but a different kind of useful: useful as a mode of communication to a mass, implicitly conscious-of-advertising audience."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.293

Reference work: *Designs for Fragile Personalities in Anxious Times: Haggable Atomic Mushroom* (2004/5), with Michael Anantanand

**The MSCHF divergence from Dunne & Raby:**
- Dunne & Raby: speculative design objects stay in museum collections
- MSCHF: speculative design objects enter the actual marketplace and are activated by real communities

> "Jesus Shoes was meant to live in its actual ecosystem, far beyond MSCHF's original scope."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.293

MSCHF took the academic framework of speculative design and made it operational — selling real objects in real markets to real audiences who engage with both the product and the critique simultaneously.

**Confidence:** HIGH [Book p.293]
