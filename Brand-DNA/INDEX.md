# MSCHF Brand DNA

> Team structure, decision-making, hiring, what makes them work.
> Extracted from 12 transcripts (2019–2025) + *Made by MSCHF* (Phaidon, 2024). All claims cited.
> Confidence: HIGH = direct quote, MEDIUM = inferred from pattern, LOW = single mention

---

## Core Self-Definition (Master Quote)

> "A streetwear brand designed to function like a missile: a container for cultural desires lobbed into the social media attention sphere."
> — Kevin Wiesner, *Made by MSCHF* [Book p.63], **Confidence: HIGH**

This is the clearest single-sentence description of MSCHF's fundamental architecture, stated by a co-founder/co-author of the book:
- **The missile** = the drop (the object/experience)
- **The container** = the cultural material inside (the concept, the emotion, the provocation)
- **Cultural desires** = the pre-loaded meaning borrowed from the cultural ready-made
- **Lobbed** = the distro strategy — aimed and released, not broadcast
- **The social media attention sphere** = the medium (not the platform — the attention itself)

Companion quote from Gabe Whaley on the same idea:
> "It's me and 30 of my friends here in Brooklyn. And we've basically created a wedge in the universe that allows us to make whatever we want, whenever we want, that somehow conveys our point of view in ways that are actually interactive in the systems that we're commenting on."
> — Gabe Whaley, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*, **Confidence: HIGH**

## What MSCHF Actually Is

MSCHF resists definition on purpose. But across all transcripts, a consistent picture emerges:

> "It's me and 30 of my friends here in Brooklyn. And we've basically created a wedge in the universe that allows us to make whatever we want, whenever we want, that somehow conveys our point of view in ways that are actually interactive in the systems that we're commenting on."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

> "Mischief is whatever is most convenient for you to describe it as at the time... in court in front of a judge it behooves mischief to 100% unabashedly be an artistic entity. The convenient thing is that it is also true."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Formal structure:** For-profit art collective. Represented by Perrotin Gallery. Works in museums worldwide. Also sells physical goods, software, and luxury items. Raised VC money from Silicon Valley.

**Current structure (2025):** Holding company of creative enterprises. Each category (footwear, luxury leather goods, fine art, potentially theme parks) gets its own sub-brand, team, budget, P&L. Example: GSCT by MSCHF (handbag line).

**Confidence:** HIGH

## Team Size and Composition

**Size:** Under 30 people (confirmed multiple times, 2024–2025)
> "The company is under 30 people. We have just enough people in each like core discipline to be able to like excel at every category."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Composition:** Deliberately cross-disciplinary. Not just creatives.
> "There are probably fewer than I can count on one hand people who had established careers before Mischief. Everyone here like the majority of people here were very early in their careers or this is like job number one for them truly."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Departments:**
- Ideation (everyone participates)
- The Librarian (full-time input curator — see below)
- Distro (distribution / viral loop design)
- Design (4-person production team that makes everything)
- Engineering / product
- Manufacturing
- Legal (added after repeated lawsuits)
- Finance
- Customer support
- HR

> "A four person production team that makes everything from tax software to various footwear — they have to be unbelievably agile."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## The Librarian Role

One full-time person whose job is to feed raw material into the Mischief Machine. Unique to MSCHF. Not a creative director — an input curator.

> "We have one person whose full-time job is essentially, it's very similar to being a university professor or a high school teacher. We refer to them as the librarian. Their job is to curate topics, build a curriculum, do research, and present new inputs for the Mischief Machine. And that comes in the form of white papers, mood boards, presentations, guest lectures, talks, anything that you can imagine, just like you would see in school. Those are circulated on a weekly basis to the team."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## Hiring Philosophy

**Core filter:** Devil-may-care attitude. The ability to laugh your way through catastrophe.

> "The through line for everyone that we hire is they have to sort of have this like Devil-may-care attitude... no matter how bad it gets you're probably going to laugh your way through it right... it's going to be fine."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**Outsider profile:** Not the cool kid. Not fashionable or tapped-in. Just quiet nerds who love making things.

> "It's a very similar makeup of people who were not the center of attention in school growing up. They were not the cool kids. They're not particularly fashionable or tapped in. They are just honestly quiet nerds who love making things."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHIEF, an Art and Discipline*

**Track record of making things independently.** Tools now exist for everyone — find the people already using them.

> "I would be finding finding the kids who are making stuff and like seeing around the corners."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Not too precious about the work.**

> "I like that they're very chill very nice like kind. They're not too cool for school... the people here aren't trying to like show up for like the photos."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Mistake-making as job requirement (early rule):**
> "If we have an employee who doesn't make a royal mistake in like the first three months of them working with us I have to sit down and have a talk and be like I don't think you're triggering enough of your co-workers."
> — Gabe, *_24 Feb 2020 Hacking Culture*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## Decision-Making

**Flat and fast.** No layers. Candor replaces committees.

> "We've wiped away a lot of the bureaucracy... there's just so much candor here and like quick honesty and like ability to make fast decisions."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Instinct-based (on ideas):**
> "If you spend too much time deliberating on it, you're wrong."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**Data: track but don't celebrate.** MSCHF ruthlessly tracks open rates, sellout times, resell multiples, video views — but internally it's forbidden to use that data as the emotional measure of success.

> "We would ruthlessly track open rates, sellout times, resell multiples, page views, video views on content that other people make. That was a very, very important part. Couldn't celebrate that though."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Worst practices protected.** Finance wants best practices. MSCHF protects space for financially irrational projects because they produce cultural returns that compound.

> "There's like a target of money that you need to make to be able to do like bigger things that just don't make money but are so cool that you have to do them."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## Cultural Position: The Permanent Outsider

MSCHF consciously maintains outsider status across every sphere they enter — fine art, streetwear, luxury, tech, collectibles.

> "There is a pull for mischief to like start to be more embedded into that institution into that scene and to start becoming a part of it and then to ultimately start creating output for that crowd — and I think that's the biggest trap."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

The origin story matters: Gabe grew up in rural North Carolina, no media, no culture, classical piano only, West Point, quit, bummed around, accidentally learned to code building joke websites. This outsider trajectory is not a liability — it's the lens.

> "I went from this very sheltered, strict upbringing where I was not allowed to watch TV, movies, play video games, listen to popular music... I literally knew nothing about culture... And somehow that manifested in creating these projects that critiqued everything that I was observing because I wasn't able to participate in it."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## The For-Profit Art Collective Tension

MSCHF is genuinely both things — it's not a performance of being an art collective, and it's not a performance of being a business. The tension is real and productive.

> "We are like so aggressively commercial that it makes you question like what is the intention of all of this in the first place right — are they a brand trying to make money, are they a business trying to like take your money, is it an artist trying to like make a statement? Mischief is just my excuse and my friend's excuse to make all the ideas that are in our heads, make them real, and put them out into the world."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Represented by:** Perrotin Gallery (Paris/global). Works in museums worldwide.

**Raised:** VC money from Silicon Valley. Used "tech media play" framing.

**Revenue model:** Product sales (drops), resell ecosystem, licensing/collaborations, Applied Mischief (agency, 2025), fine art sales.

**Confidence:** HIGH

## The Friday Hackathon (Origin Practice)

Before the formalized brainstorm process, Gabe ran "Mischief Madness" — a monthly Friday hackathon after realizing the siloed company structure was killing creativity.

> "I show up in the office and I say okay we're transitioning to 4-day workweeks. We are now working on Fridays but show up no laptops — I'm bringing breakfast and bringing lunch and we're gonna embark on this one-month experiment called Mischief Madness... 30 minutes over bagels slinging insights just sharing stories along a theme, then we break off into pairs... come back and then we have lunch while doing demo day which is more like stand-up comedy than an actual pitch."
> — Gabe, *_24 Feb 2020 Hacking Culture*

> "Team unity has exploded... our ideas are actually better... it kept me from shutting down the company last year."
> — Gabe, *_24 Feb 2020 Hacking Culture*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## The Korean Retrospective (International Proof)

In 2023, a Korean museum reached out for a full retrospective of every MSCHF work. Opening night broke the museum's attendance record. K-pop stars showed up. Cast of Squid Game attended. No localization was done.

> "I don't think it's about Asia versus like the United States versus like any geographical distinction. I think what I took away from that is like there is actually like a shared international language of creativity... all those kids that were showing up at that Museum opening had so much in common actually with the kids that show up at our New York opening."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## What Makes It Work (Summary)

1. **Outsider DNA baked into every hire** — never the cool kid, always the nerd
2. **Small team, radical trust** — under 30 people, candor replaces bureaucracy
3. **The Librarian** — dedicated input curation so brainstorm starts from raw material not trend reaction
4. **Daily creative training** — 1-hour brainstorm every weekday, every idea documented
5. **Protected financial irrationality** — space budgeted for projects that lose money but build culture
6. **Distro as a discipline** — viral loops designed into every product before production
7. **Anti-insider immune system** — the pull toward acceptance is actively resisted
8. **Pre-release celebration** — decouples emotional identity from audience reception

**Confidence:** HIGH across all eight

## The Book as a Drop (Archive #202)

*Made By MSCHF* (Phaidon, 2024) — the physical monograph — is itself **Archive #202** in MSCHF's own catalog. The book archives itself.

This is not a footnote. It's a structural statement about how MSCHF thinks about documentation:
- The act of compiling and publishing a record of your work is itself a work
- The book exists in the same archive as the ATM, the shoes, the handbags
- Documentation = production = drop

**Confidence:** HIGH [Book p.270, Project Archive table]

## Design Lineage: Dunne & Raby (Speculative Design)

MSCHF explicitly cites **Anthony Dunne and Fiona Raby** as a direct design influence — specifically in the context of Jesus Shoes (Archive #7, 2019):

> "Speculative design principles assert that design is also useful — but a different kind of useful: useful as a mode of communication to a mass, implicitly conscious-of-advertising audience."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.293

Reference work: *Designs for Fragile Personalities in Anxious Times: Haggable Atomic Mushroom* (2004/5), with Michael Anantanand

**The MSCHF divergence from Dunne & Raby:**
- Dunne & Raby: speculative design objects stay in museum collections
- MSCHF: speculative design objects enter the actual marketplace and are activated by real communities

> "Jesus Shoes was meant to live in its actual ecosystem, far beyond MSCHF's original scope."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.293

MSCHF took the academic framework of speculative design and made it operational — selling real objects in real markets to real audiences who engage with both the product and the critique simultaneously.

**Confidence:** HIGH [Book p.293]
