# MSCHF Creative Process

> Full methodology: how ideas start, how they're tested, how they're built, how they're released.
> Extracted from 12 transcripts (2019–2025). All claims cited.

---

## Phase 0: Raw Material (The Librarian)

Before any brainstorm happens, one full-time person curates inputs.

> "We have one person whose full-time job is essentially like being a university professor or a high school teacher. We refer to them as the librarian. Their job is to curate topics, build a curriculum, do research, and present new inputs for the Mischief Machine. And that comes in the form of white papers, mood boards, presentations, guest lectures, talks, anything that you can imagine. Those are circulated on a weekly basis to the team."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Why it matters:** Ideas don't start from "what's trending." They start from curated material — cultural, academic, technical. This is what prevents MSCHF from being reactive.

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## Phase 1: Daily Brainstorm

Every weekday. One hour. Multiple formats.

> "Every single day, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, there is a one-hour long brainstorm of various formats. It can be group work. It can be heads down solo. It can be drawing. You have to instill a lot of variety into that."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

> "Everyone here is an idea person. The lawyer or general counsel joins brainstorms, our ops lead joins brainstorms, because being creative doesn't mean you have good ideas — being creative just means you're human."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*

**What gets generated:** Thousands and thousands of ideas. Most are terrible.

> "I think we have you know thousands and thousands of unrealized ideas and Concepts that we've come up with over the years now. And most of them are terrible."
> — Kevin, *_19 Mar 2024 BUSINESS OF HYPE*

**Every idea is documented, tagged, categorized.** There's a large database/library of concepts discussed for years.

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## Phase 2: The Ice Box (Test of Time)

Ideas that survive initial brainstorm go into a pool. Then they're deliberately ignored.

> "We stow it away for many months and then we revisit it later. If it still hits for us, then we will start committing resources to doing it."
> — Gabe, *15 Oct 2025 outlasting hype*

> "We forced the idea to sit in that pool for another couple of months. Now it has to survive the test of time. You forget about it, and you come back to it. And if it still feels exciting, then you slot it for production."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

> "Ideas have to sit in the pool for months at a time and every time we revisit the pool to add new ideas you have to kill old ones."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**Vetting criteria:**
- Does it conceptually represent what we care about right now?
- Is it doable? Can we afford it?
- Can we distribute it?
- Does it still excite us 2–6 months later?

**Timeframe:** 2–6 months in the pool before green-light.

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## Phase 3: Green Light → Production (12 Months Out)

Once green-lit, production slots are typically 12 months away. Projects are never made in 2 weeks despite appearing that way.

> "Projects are like a year out at this point in time so like we're working on things if not longer. So it's always shocking to me when someone says oh yeah you guys made that in two weeks and we're like no no no this has been in the works for a long time."
> — Kevin, *_19 Mar 2024 BUSINESS OF HYPE*

> "A full-on product that we cut tooling for — there is no way we made this in two weeks."
> — Kevin, *_19 Mar 2024 BUSINESS OF HYPE*

**Production speed (once in motion):** Extremely fast. Packaging design that would take a funded startup 9 months and a branding agency gets done in 2 weeks.

> "We do in two weeks. That tax software packaging design that would take another company nine months — we do in two weeks."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## Phase 4: Distro (Distribution Design)

Before release, the Distro department reverse-engineers how the idea will spread. Viral loops are designed in, not hoped for.

> "Distro is obsessed with how do you plant opportunities for natural circulation and maybe even virality... building in loops like viral shareable loops into the production process itself and then marketing it."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**The 3-Moment Strategy:**
> "A big part of our strategy used to be having a death strategy for every product that we would do because that would give you another moment. So there's the get excited before it's real. Now it's real. Then it dies. Three moments. And if you do it right, that actually spreads your attention span over maybe a glorious two weeks, which is infinity in today's terms."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Example of Distro built into a product:**
> "What did the Distro team suggest for the Satan shoe? You get Lil Nas X to be the photo shoot model. And you also get Lil Nas X to wear them in his Montero music video. And then that creates a whole world of assets that you know are just gonna like start making the rounds."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Placement matters as much as the idea:**
> "It's not enough to do the thing, you have to figure out where to put the thing... we refer to distribution as like how does an idea spread and how do you boil that down to like the most tactile like physical essence — like where does it go, who sees it, who interacts with it in a way that gives this concept the most power to spread the idea."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**ATM Leaderboard + Art Basel:** Cold-called the head of North America for Art Basel. Formed a fake gallery ("Mischief Gallery") to apply. Eventually entered via Perrotin Gallery relationship. Put the ATM machine at the most wealth-concentrated art fair on earth because that was the only context where it could reach full power.

**Confidence:** HIGH

## Phase 5: Release and Pre-Release Celebration

MSCHF celebrates the night before a drop goes out — never after.

> "Our tradition was always celebrate the night before something goes out. Don't celebrate afterwards. You have to be at peace with yourself if no one sees it. You have to be at peace if people hate it."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Seeding:** Leaking images through friends' accounts, without instruction to post. (Big Red Boots strategy.)

> "We leaked this image through a friend's Instagram account. Again, just hoping that people don't hate it, or even worse, that they don't ignore it."
> — Gabe, *TED TALK*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## The Idea Quality Test

**The One Sentence + Three Sentence Rule:**
> "A good idea has to hit in one sentence and hit even harder in three sentences. What that means is there are layers of comprehension. And typically those layers means it applies to multiple audiences."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**The Eureka Pattern:**
When a good idea is heard, it feels obvious — like it already should exist.
> "When you see it exist, it makes sense. It's intuitive or obvious. You get why this should exist."
> — Gabe, *15 Oct 2025 outlasting hype* (on Tax Heaven 3000)

**Instant gut test on new ideas:**
> "If you spend too much time deliberating on it, you're wrong."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## What MSCHF Never Does

**Never reacts to yesterday's news:**
> "Mischief doesn't do any zeitgeisty ideas, but they feel zeitgeisty. We chose an approach that speaks to much longer term behaviors and trends that feel very of the moment. We're not like Saturday Night Live."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Never chases a format:**
> "Mischief doesn't win as a first mover. If you look at all of our work it was never about being the first to react to something or the first to make use of a format."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*

**Never does holiday marketing:**
> "I don't like doing things for holiday... look at all the white space when people aren't trying to talk. That's where you can have permission to be really creative because it's wide open."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Never rushes into an overnight trend:**
> "That is the thing that like Mischief actually never does — follow like an overnight Trend... typically I mean we're at the point where we're creating trends that other people follow."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## Early Process (2016–2020)

Before the formalized system, MSCHF operated on pure velocity and a single rule: ship every 2 weeks, no matter what.

> "The core handful of tenants for Mischief was just we are going to make something every two weeks no matter what — even if it sucked we had to put it out — and we are only going to make things that we all really want to make."
> — Kevin, *_19 Mar 2024 BUSINESS OF HYPE*

> "The Mischief thing of every two weeks we have to put something out like took a little bit of that preciousness away that I think was incredibly helpful for continuing to have a creative practice that develops."
> — Lucas, *_19 Mar 2024 BUSINESS OF HYPE*

**The Framework (Casper/early era):**
> "Step one: identify what the social/emotional problem is — really both components. Step two: map a solution that doesn't necessarily solve the problem but it addresses the problem. Step three: get it out there."
> — Gabe, *_24 Feb 2020 Hacking Culture*

**The Venmo Audience (early distribution hack):**
> "Our first audience portal was Venmo. We had a huge audience on Venmo... every time we had a new project we would Venmo our audience a penny and they would get that notification because that's the most powerful notification layer in your phone — the cha-ching. I am now banned from Venmo for life."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*

**Confidence:** HIGH

## Process Summary (2025 Version)

```
WEEKLY: Librarian curates inputs → circulates white papers, mood boards, guest lectures

DAILY (Mon–Fri): 1-hour brainstorm → every idea documented + tagged

ONGOING: Mass pool of ideas → vetting (conceptually right? doable? distributable?)
         ↓
         Ice box (2–6 months ignored, then revisited)
         ↓
         Green light → Distro designs viral loops into product
         ↓
         Production (12 months out)
         ↓
         Pre-release celebration (night before)
         ↓
         Leak → Build tension → Release → Death strategy
         ↓
         Audience carries it across finish line
```
