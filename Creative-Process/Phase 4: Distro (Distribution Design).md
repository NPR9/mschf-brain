## Phase 4: Distro (Distribution Design)

Before release, the Distro department reverse-engineers how the idea will spread. Viral loops are designed in, not hoped for.

> "Distro is obsessed with how do you plant opportunities for natural circulation and maybe even virality... building in loops like viral shareable loops into the production process itself and then marketing it."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**The 3-Moment Strategy:**
> "A big part of our strategy used to be having a death strategy for every product that we would do because that would give you another moment. So there's the get excited before it's real. Now it's real. Then it dies. Three moments. And if you do it right, that actually spreads your attention span over maybe a glorious two weeks, which is infinity in today's terms."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Example of Distro built into a product:**
> "What did the Distro team suggest for the Satan shoe? You get Lil Nas X to be the photo shoot model. And you also get Lil Nas X to wear them in his Montero music video. And then that creates a whole world of assets that you know are just gonna like start making the rounds."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Placement matters as much as the idea:**
> "It's not enough to do the thing, you have to figure out where to put the thing... we refer to distribution as like how does an idea spread and how do you boil that down to like the most tactile like physical essence — like where does it go, who sees it, who interacts with it in a way that gives this concept the most power to spread the idea."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**ATM Leaderboard + Art Basel:** Cold-called the head of North America for Art Basel. Formed a fake gallery ("Mischief Gallery") to apply. Eventually entered via Perrotin Gallery relationship. Put the ATM machine at the most wealth-concentrated art fair on earth because that was the only context where it could reach full power.
