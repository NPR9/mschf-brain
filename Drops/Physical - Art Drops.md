## Physical / Art Drops

### Jesus Shoes
**Year:** 2019
**Concept:** Nike Air Max 97s injected with holy water from the River Jordan in the air bubbles. 12 pairs (one per disciple). Priced at $1,425 (Matthew 14:25 — where Jesus walked on water). Sold out immediately, made many more.
**Quote:** "We went down the street and bought these white Nike Air Max 97s... and took a needle from a hospital, don't ask how he got that, and injected water into the air bubbles... let's price them at $1,425 because Matthew 14:25 in the Bible is where Jesus walked on water." — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*
**Quote:** "Watching the different layers of appreciation — the Nike sneakerhead type people... then the Christian sneakerheads... then the legal academic professionals... some of them we were making fun of." — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*
**Principles:** [[Cultural Ready-Made]], [[One Sentence + Three]], [[Humor as Disarmament]], [[Audience Completes the Work]]
**Confidence:** HIGH

### Possibly Real Fairies by Andy Warhol by MSCHF
**Year:** ~2021
**Concept:** Bought an original Andy Warhol ink drawing of two angels for ~$30K. Forged it 999 times using a custom machine. Shuffled the original into the stack. Sold all 1,000 for $250 each. No one knows which is real — but everyone has a Mischief.
**Quote:** "We bought a original Andy Warhol ink drawing of like two angels and we built a machine and a process that allowed us to like Forge it very very well... we took the original and we shuffled it in and then completely lost it... then we sold all of them." — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

### Key4All (PT Cruiser)
**Year:** 2022
**Concept:** Bought a 2004 PT Cruiser with wood paneling. Duplicated the RFID key frequency 5,000 times. Sold 5,000 working keys to people in all 50 states. Dumped the car under the BQE. Let the crowd do whatever.
**Quote:** "We had made 5,000 keys for this car which we then sold to people in all 50 states... and we dumped the car under the BQE... and it now belonged to the crowd. Basically what we did is we rebuilt Zipcar if they only had one car." — Kevin, *_19 Mar 2024 BUSINESS OF HYPE*
**Quote:** "That car went from New York went across the entire country to California... for about 9 months before the engine ultimately burned out." — Kevin, *_19 Mar 2024 BUSINESS OF HYPE*
**Quote:** "Purchasers of the key had flown in from all over the country, not just to see the thing that they had touched and interacted with show up in a gallery, but they were actually there to meet each other for the first time." — Gabe, *TED TALK*
**Principles:** [[Audience Completes the Work]], [[Cultural Ready-Made]], [[Spicy Present]], [[Scale as Force Multiplier]]
**Confidence:** HIGH

### ATM Leaderboard
**Year:** 2022 (Art Basel Miami)
**Concept:** Functional ATM machine with a digital leaderboard ranking people by bank balance. Cold-called Art Basel to get in. Wealthy collectors avoided it on Day 1. Day 3–5: massive crowds, security guards added, fire exits blocked. People with $2 in their accounts became crowd heroes. Sold as sculpture for $75K. Diplo clocked $3M.
**Quote:** "The artwork was not the ATM machine. The artwork was the act of people engaging with the ATM machine. The actual artwork was the relationships that people developed with one another via the ATM machine." — Gabe, *TED TALK*
**Quote:** "Someone with $37 in their account would swipe, get ranked at the bottom, turn around to face the audience, and the audience would erupt in applause... it became sort of like a 'one of us' celebration." — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*
**Principles:** [[Audience Completes the Work]], [[Distribution Is a Design Discipline]] (Art Basel was the venue), [[Spicy Present]], [[Cultural Ready-Made]]
**Confidence:** HIGH

### Big Red Boots
**Year:** 2023
**Concept:** Life-size cartoon boots made real. Inspired by comic book characters. $350. Originally made 200–300 pairs. Went viral via a single mirror-pic someone posted. Spawned massive fakes market. Used as Meta's case study for virality.
**Quote:** "We sketched the shoe and it's obviously inspired from like certain comic book characters but we're like but what if it was real... when we made the first prototype one of my colleagues put them on and started like plopping around... and we're like it actually looks pretty good." — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*
**Quote:** "We priced them at $350 and we just prayed that there would be a few hundred people out there in the world who would spend money on these crazy looking things." — Gabe, *TED TALK*
**Quote:** "It was like Rambo on the top of a mountain... within an hour we had over 100,000 orders. That's crazy for a $350 shoe." — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*
**Principles:** [[Spicy Present]], [[Emotion First]], [[Distribution Is a Design Discipline]], [[Test of Time]]

### Microscopic Handbags
**Archive #:** 157
**Year:** 2023
**Format:** Microscale sculpture / product
**Concept:** Handbags physically smaller than a grain of sand, made via two-photon polymerization 3D printing (biomedical technology). Brands: Hermès, Jacquemus, Gucci, Louis Vuitton. Packaged in semiconductor gel-pak cases: "THIS SIDE UP / USE MICROSCOPE." Version 1: product edition. Version 2: gallery microscope stands. Sold at auction at Perrotin for $63,750.
**Quote:** "We made a microscopic handbag that sold at auction for $63,000 at Paris Fashion Week at Perel's... making it the world's most expensive handbag per volume. That idea came from a summer intern." — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*
**Legal:** Hermès v. Rothschild (Mason Rothschild NFT Birkin case) observed as precedent; First Amendment parody protection applied.
**Exhibition:** "Nothing Is Sacred," Daelim Museum, Seoul, 2023 — three microscope stands on pedestals

### Global Supply Chain Telephone
**Archive #:** 179
**Year:** 2024
**Concept:** Played telephone with a handbag design across 7 factories in 6 countries. Each factory received the previous sample + a new directional prompt (Birkin → Saddle → Hourglass → ...). No predetermined endpoint. The finished bag is whatever the chain produced — design generated by the system, not by MSCHF. See [[Drops/global-supply-chain-telephone]] for full page.
**Quote:** "We played the game of telephone but with the global supply chain of handbag manufacturers... information is being Lost in Translation but also the factories are actually like very creative." — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*
**Quote:** "Unlike other Absurd System projects (Key4All), GSCT lacked a predetermined endpoint." — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.206
**Principles:** [[Exploit Existing Infrastructure]], [[Cultural Ready-Made]], [[Audience Completes the Work]] (factories as co-creators), [[Real and Fake Are Made in the Same Factory]]
**Confidence:** HIGH [Book p.201–215 + Transcripts]

### Met Sink (Ship of Theseus)
**Year:** ~2023
**Concept:** Applied the Ship of Theseus paradox to a sink in the American Wing of the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Identified the sink, ordered every part by serial number, built an identical replica, then over many months swapped one piece at a time until they had the Met's sink and the Met had MSCHF's. MSCHF text carved into pipe fittings — technically a piece in the Met.
**Quote:** "Over many months we would go into the Met one piece at a time swap it out until by the end we had their sink and in the Met is a sink that's identical to the one that was there but is the Mischief sink." — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*
**Quote:** "The idea of a Mischief artwork living in the Met but not living in a gallery — living in a bathroom — is so perfect for Mischief." — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*
**Quote:** "We fabricated a wheelchair that could have hid it in the seat but... we got a little scared, we got a little carried away." — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*
**Principles:** [[Cultural Ready-Made]], [[Black Box]], [[Humor as Disarmament]], [[Worst Practices Are Essential]]
**Confidence:** HIGH

### MSCHF Socks (Costco Pallet)
**Year:** ~2020–2021
**Concept:** Instead of selling socks individually, sold a pallet of 1,000 MSCHF socks (Costco-style) for $5,000. Whoever bought it first owned the supply and controlled the market. A woman in Florida accidentally got it at 2am during a test — and ended up 3x-ing her money selling them via Reddit, eBay, StockX, and sending her kid to school with socks to sell.
**Quote:** "Instead of selling one pair of socks at a time, we bundled them into a pallet of a thousand socks for five grand. The idea was, whoever buys it first owns the supply. They control the market. Let's see what they do." — Gabe, *15 Oct 2025 outlasting hype*
**Principles:** [[Audience Completes the Work]], [[Humor as Disarmament]], [[Cultural Ready-Made]]
**Confidence:** HIGH

### Spot's Rampage
**Archive #:** 58
**Year:** 2021
**Format:** Remote-controlled robot / interactive performance
**Concept:** Purchased a Boston Dynamics Spot robot ($74,500 retail). Mounted a paintball gun. Livestreamed full remote piloting to public on February 24, 2021 — first public remote piloting of Spot in the world. Control transferred to random viewer every 2 minutes. Boston Dynamics preemptively condemned the drop before launch (Streisand Effect). NYPD then deployed Spot robots in the Bronx, confirming MSCHF's critique. Boston Dynamics remotely disabled the robot via software update months later.
**Quote:** "We managed to get one. Instead of making it dance, we strapped a paintball gun to it, and we connected it remotely to a website where people could take turns driving it and firing it in an art gallery that we constructed. Boston Dynamics did not like that one very much." — Gabe, *TED TALK*
**Quote:** "To our delight, they had responded to something that hadn't happened yet, providing the best possible publicity." — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.317 (on BD's preemptive condemnation)
**Aftermath:** Disabled Spot became "Spot's Revenge" (Archive #120) at Perrotin NY 2022 — decorated with Gundam-style resin gun assemblage
**Principles:** [[Audience Completes the Work]], [[Always Punch Something Never Punch Down]], [[Do the Bit Until They Take the Bait]], [[Death Is Designed, Not Suffered]]
**Confidence:** HIGH [Book p.311–321 + Transcripts]

### Infinite Relic
**Archive #:** 54
**Year:** 2021
**Format:** Religious object / biotech artwork
**Concept:** Replicated a Catholic gold monstrance with a thermometer replacing the sacred host. Interior contains actual blood from a canonized saint — a missionary martyred in 1853, frozen in the Andes, blood cells still viable after 170 years. MSCHF cold-chained the blood from Italy to Brooklyn, grew it in vitro to produce 47 liters total, distributed as 500 limited relics. Tagline: "Sanguis Populi" (Blood of the People). TikTok: "drink a holy blood relic with me" — 2.7M views.
**Key mechanism:** "Infinite Relic achieved the multiplication of a relic, not its division." — John Calvin's 1534 critique of relic proliferation used as historical framework.
**Historical ref:** John Calvin, *Treatise on Relics* (1534) — observed that for claimed relics to be authentic, "every apostle would have needed more than four bodies"
**Principles:** [[Exploit Existing Infrastructure]], [[Do the Bit Until They Take the Bait]], [[Cultural Ready-Made]], [[Strong Associations Are Free Strong Reactions]]
**Confidence:** HIGH [Book p.335–345]
