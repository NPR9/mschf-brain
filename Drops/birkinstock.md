# Birkinstock

**Archive #:** 47
**Year:** 2021
**Format:** Custom luxury object / cultural ready-made
**Source:** *Made by MSCHF* Book p.111 + Project Archive confirmed
**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## One-Line Concept

Destroyed a Hermès Birkin 35 and reconstructed it as a Birkenstock sandal — the Birkin's handle becomes the arch support, the body becomes the footbed.

---

## What It Does

Two of the most recognizable luxury objects in fashion — one hyperluxury (Hermès Birkin, $10K+), one anti-luxury (Birkenstock, functional/practical) — merged into a single hybrid object. The Birkin's structure is physically deconstructed and rebuilt into the sandal form.

Neither a Birkin nor a Birkenstock survives intact. The result is both and neither.

---

## Cultural Logic

| Object | Cultural Association |
|--------|---------------------|
| Hermès Birkin | Maximum luxury, waiting lists, investment asset, status signal |
| Birkenstock | Anti-fashion, comfort-first, hippie/academic, deliberate anti-status |

Combining them produces an object that carries both sets of associations simultaneously — which cancels neither but makes both visible. The "luxury" reading and the "anti-luxury" reading exist in permanent tension.
