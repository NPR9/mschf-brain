# Global Supply Chain Telephone (GSCT)

**Archive #:** 179
**Year:** 2024
**Format:** Handbag / manufacturing concept
**Source:** *Made by MSCHF* Book p.201–215 (full Absurd Systems documentation) + Transcripts
**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## One-Line Concept

Played telephone with a handbag design across seven factories in six countries — each factory received the previous factory's output as its prompt. Result: a luxury bag that embodies supply chain as material.

---

## Full Description

MSCHF selected a Hermès Birkin bag as the starting point and gave the following brief to Factory 1 (Peru): *knock off a Hermès Birkin bag*. Factory 1's output became Factory 2's input — along with a new instruction: *make the bag more like the Dior Saddle bag*. Each subsequent factory received the previous factory's physical sample plus a new directional prompt pushing the design toward a different luxury house silhouette.

By the final factory (China), the design had mutated through five distinct luxury-bag DNA sources. The finished GSCT handbag is a completely non-traditional form — no single luxury house is identifiable. It embodies the entire supply chain as a design artifact.

**The game is the telephone. The bag is the artifact of that game.**

---

## Factory Chain

| Step | Country | Prompt Given |
|------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Peru | Knock off a Hermès Birkin bag |
| 2 | Portugal | Make the bag designed by Factory 1 more like the Dior Saddle bag |
| 3 | China | Make the bag designed by Factory 2 more like the Balenciaga Hourglass bag |
| 4 | India | [evolved prompt — further mutation parameters] |
| 5–7 | Multiple | [continued telephone chain] |

**Total:** 7 factories, 6 countries (US, Peru, Portugal, India, China, Mexico), approximately 2 weeks

## Genesis

> "Factories contribute a tremendous amount of creative work behind the scenes. MSCHF has repeatedly found that factories often surprise us, especially when they were not allowed to do so."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.203

Unlike all other Absurd System projects (Key4All had a destination — Perrotin LA; Tax Heaven 3000 had a form — Form 1040), GSCT had **no predetermined endpoint**. MSCHF provided settings and parameters, then followed the chain wherever it led.

The final bag was not designed by MSCHF. It was generated by the manufacturing system itself.

---

## Two Factory Modes (Key Finding)

MSCHF discovered two distinct factory engagement modes, each producing different creative results:

| Mode | Setup | Result |
|------|-------|--------|
| Informed | Factory knows the full GSCT concept | Deliberate, contextually aware creative decisions |
| Uninformed | Factory receives only the previous sample + brief | "We got a lot of clutch designs" — surprising, unconstrained creativity |

**Example:** A factory told only to "feminize" the bag — without knowing it was part of a telephone game — produced an unprompted quilted design. Another factory incorporated a USB power strip into the handle, a genuinely novel structural solution that emerged from misunderstanding the brief.

---

## Products as Vehicles for Concepts

MSCHF chose handbags specifically because of their unique position in the fashion ecosystem:

- Experimental runway clothing doesn't reach mass audiences — it only circulates among fashion insiders
- Luxury handbags CAN reach mass audiences — they're smaller, wearable daily, and aspirationally accessible
- Luxury bags are the one segment of high fashion that bridges fine art and mass market

> "Luxury bags straddle the line between function and non-function in the fashion industry."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.209

## Related Drop: Made In Italy (Archive #142, 2022)

Documented in the GSCT Absurd Systems section as a companion project on manufacturing authenticity:

- Briefcase-sized leather bags manufactured in **Italy, Texas** (2,000-person ghost town, no existing fabrication infrastructure)
- "ITALY" embossed on main faces; "TEXAS" embossed on bottom
- MSCHF loaded a 20-foot truck with all equipment, drove to Italy TX, hired craftspeople from surrounding region
- Price: $650. Dimensions: 28 × 34 × 8.1 cm.

> "Marketing terms such as 'Made in Italy,' 'Made in America,' and 'Made in China' are outdated. In twenty-first-century commodity production, there is no such thing as a single country of origin."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.212

**The critique:** "Made in Italy" as a luxury signifier is pure fiction. MSCHF made that fiction literal by manufacturing in Italy, TX — technically accurate, categorically deceptive.

## Key Contrast: GSCT vs. Key4All

Both are Absurd Systems. Both involve distributed participation. But structurally opposite:

| | Key4All (#118) | GSCT (#179) |
|--|----------------|-------------|
| Participants | 5,000 key buyers | 7 factories |
| Endpoint | Predetermined (Perrotin LA) | None — wherever chain leads |
| Creative agency | Community self-governance | Factory misinterpretation |
| Output | Car's journey + community | Mutated handbag |
| Scale | 54.6M TikTok views | Fine art/luxury market |
