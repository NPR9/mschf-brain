# Jesus Shoes

**Archive #:** 7
**Year:** 2019
**Format:** Custom sneaker / cultural ready-made
**Source:** *Made by MSCHF* Book p.272–293 (Absurd Objects section) + Transcripts (4 mentions)
**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## One-Line Concept

Off-the-shelf Nike Air Max 97s injected with holy water from the River Jordan — so you can walk on water. 12 pairs. $1,425 each (Matthew 14:25). Sold in under one minute.

---

## Full Description

Jesus Shoes are white Nike Air Max 97s customized with:
- **Holy water** from the Jordan River injected into the air bubble soles (allowing the wearer to "walk on water")
- **Frankincense-scented wood insole** (referencing the Lamb of God and the gifts of the Magi)
- **Graphic reading "MT 14:25"** (Matthew 14:25 — the chapter and verse where Jesus walks on water)
- **"INRI" branding** on the tongue
- **Cross detail** on the laces

These were the **first physical objects MSCHF ever produced** — Archive #7, 2019.

---

## What MSCHF Was Targeting

> "We set out to parody paradigms emerging in mainstream commerce in 2019. Brand collaborations were spiraling into self-satirizing ubiquity, where all commodities were fungible. Any two brands or pieces of IP could be seamlessly and productively combined — a consumerist liquid diet, the equivalent of a green smoothie."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.276

The target was **2019 brand-collab culture** — the endless parade of luxury×streetwear, brand×artist, IP×IP mashups that had drained all meaning from collaboration. Jesus Shoes weaponized that format by combining Nike with the most overdetermined IP in Western civilization.

> "Commodities are building blocks in this practice, but so are the brands or corporations producing them. Nike would be MSCHF's contemporary equivalent. The porcelain urinal was Duchamp's ready-made material; the Kohler company would be MSCHF's contemporary equivalent."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.276

Nike = Duchamp's Kohler. The brand itself is the material.

---

## The Recombinant Process (Design Principle)

> "Jesus Shoes established a recombinant process that has become integral to MSCHF's work. The physical objects were made using a commercially available product as raw material. Moreover, MSCHF consciously played on the commercialization of brands as a warped mirror reflection of ready-made approaches to art."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.276

This is the **founding statement of MSCHF's physical object practice**:
1. Take commercially available product (Nike Air Max 97)
2. Use brand as material (Nike's meaning = the Kohler urinal)
3. Add conceptually loaded modification (holy water, biblical reference)
4. Release at price point that signals meaning ($1,425 = Matthew 14:25)

Every subsequent physical drop follows this recombinant logic.

---

## Design Theory Lineage

MSCHF cites **Anthony Dunne and Fiona Raby** as direct design influence — specifically their piece:

> *Designs for Fragile Personalities in Anxious Times: Haggable Atomic Mushroom* (2004/5), with Michael Anantanand
> Inflatable latex and polyester stuffing; 120 × 170 × 65 cm

Dunne & Raby's speculative design principle: design can be "useful as a mode of communication to a mass, implicitly conscious-of-advertising audience" — not just functional, but critically functional.

> "Mushroom is a speculative product, pointing that a consumer can buy 'acceptance' of a situation to alleviate an existential fear; however the work resides exclusively in the collections of museums; Jesus Shoes was meant to live in its actual ecosystem, far beyond MSCHF's original scope."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.293

**The key distinction:** Dunne & Raby's work stays in museums. Jesus Shoes entered the actual marketplace and was activated by the communities it targeted. Speculative design made operational.

## Production

- **12 pairs only** — original edition
- **Price:** $1,425 (Matthew 14:25)
- **Size:** 12 only (one per disciple)
- **Holy water injection method:** DIY hydraulic press + surgical tubing + hypodermic needles
- "We built a rough but effective hydraulic press, which automated the holy water injections via a mess of surgical tubing and hypodermic needles." — Book p.281
- "At that moment, there were no job distinctions at MSCHF — everyone simply came to make shoes and there would be no other choice."

## Launch + Scale

**Pre-launch:** StockX co-founder Josh Luber received a sample, pre-listed the shoes on StockX before launch — the first MSCHF project to receive that treatment. MSCHF didn't arrange this.

**Day of launch:**
- 100,000+ people on the Jesus Shoes website simultaneously
- Sold out in under 1 minute (12 pairs)
- Fox News chyron: "$3,000 Jesus Shoes injected with holy water from Jordan River sell out in minutes"

**Post-launch:**
- StockX resale immediately active
- Multiple press cycles: sneaker media → Christian communities → legal/academic commentary → mainstream culture
- "Watching the different layers of appreciation — the Nike sneakerhead type people... then the Christian sneakerheads... then the legal academic professionals... some of them we were making fun of." — Gabe, Transcripts

**Impact:** Jesus Shoes "dramatically escalated MSCHF's profile, and the project's success laid the foundation for our collective for the next few years." — Book p.279

---

## The Multi-Layer Audience Effect

Gabe documented the layered reception explicitly:
1. **Nike sneakerheads:** reacted to the Air Max 97 silhouette and resale potential
2. **Christian sneakerheads:** reacted to the religious content (positively)
3. **Outraged Christians:** reacted to perceived blasphemy
4. **Legal/academic observers:** reacted to the IP + religious IP mashup
5. **Art world:** reacted to the Duchamp ready-made logic

Each layer generated its own press cycle. MSCHF didn't target all five — they emerged from the structure of the drop.

## Diptych with Satan Shoes

Jesus Shoes was always conceived as half of a diptych:

> "The follow-up to that was the Satan shoe, because from an academic point of view, you can't have Jesus without Satan and vice versa. And being the nerds that we are, we knew we had to do both." — Gabe, Transcripts

The diptych was completed in 2021 with [[Drops/satan-shoes]] (Archive #65).
