# Satan Shoes

**Archive #:** 65
**Year:** 2021
**Format:** Custom sneaker / cultural ready-made / music video tie-in
**Source:** *Made by MSCHF* Book p.273–295 (Absurd Objects section) + Transcripts (5 mentions)
**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## One-Line Concept

666 black Nike Air Max 97s with red ink and human blood in the soles, made with Lil Nas X to launch alongside "MONTERO (Call Me by Your Name)" — a shoe about reclaiming satanic imagery for queer liberation. Nike sued. One person asked for a refund.

---

## Full Description

Satan Shoes are black Nike Air Max 97s customized with:
- **Red ink + human blood** in the air bubble soles (one drop per shoe)
- **Pentagram** metal charm on laces
- **"LVCIFER"** text
- **Luke 10:18** biblical reference: "I saw Satan fall like lightning from heaven"
- **666 pairs** (limited edition — the number of the beast)

Made in collaboration with **Lil Nas X**, debuting alongside his music video "MONTERO (Call Me by Your Name)" on March 26, 2021.

---

## The Diptych Logic

Satan Shoes were always planned the moment Jesus Shoes (Archive #7, 2019) succeeded:

> "From the moment Jesus Shoes became a smash hit, MSCHF planned to release Satan Shoes. Clearly this would involve black Air Max 97s with satanic imagery and materials. We had no firm plan for when any of this would happen, but it was a logical follow-up, a diptych of sorts."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.283

> "The follow-up to that was the Satan shoe, because from an academic point of view, you can't have Jesus without Satan and vice versa." — Gabe, Transcripts

The drop was conceptually complete before a collaborator was found. Lil Nas X wasn't the origin — he was the right vessel for an already-formed idea.

---

## Why Lil Nas X — The Alignment Was Not Accidental

> "In early 2021 we wound up in conversation with Lil Nas X. His team was in the middle of producing the music video for 'MONTERO (Call Me by Your Name).' Lil Nas X had just begun to make his queerness central to his public persona, and the music video heavily invoked satanic imagery as a tool to reclaim the narrative that gay people go to hell. This context was an opportunity for Satan Shoes to engage with a larger cultural narrative. We proposed Satan Shoes as a collaborative project, and Lil Nas X accepted."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.283

**The specific alignment:**
- MONTERO's video: Lil Nas X pole dances to hell, gives Satan a lap dance, kills Satan, takes his horns
- Satanic imagery used to reclaim the evangelical narrative that gay people are damned
- Satan Shoes arrived as a physical object embodying that same reclamation
- The shoe and the video shared the same argument; MSCHF provided the object; Lil Nas X provided the narrative

Satan Shoes weren't a celebrity shoe collab. They were a **shared political argument** expressed in two simultaneous media: music video + physical product.

## Production

**Scale:** Exactly 666 pairs — required precise production planning

**Production method:**
- Jesus Shoes: DIY hydraulic press + surgical tubing (improvised)
- Satan Shoes: **automated volumetric syringe pumps** from a biology lab machinery supplier

> "In contrast to the ramshackle injection system for Jesus Shoes, the Satan Shoes' pumps were automated volumetric pumps purchased from a machinery supplier for biology labs."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.286

**Factory:** Maspeth, Queens — production line trained for the run

**The blood:**
- Required human blood per shoe
- One MSCHF team member cut her hand working on the project
- She bled directly into the blood bucket for the soles while waiting for first aid

**Schedule:** Set to launch Monday March 29, 2021; production + fulfillment designed to complete within one week

## The Nike Lawsuit

**Before launch:** Nike's own social media team had been posting Satan Shoes content approvingly — until someone senior noticed and ordered it pulled. Nike's internal confusion about the shoes' origin was evidence of how real the product read.

**After launch:** Nike filed for a Temporary Restraining Order (TRO) — which, if granted, would have frozen all MSCHF operations until the lawsuit's resolution.

**MSCHF public statement** (in full):
> "The following is our official statement regarding the joint case between MSCHF and Nike. We welcome Nike's role in illuminating the context of MSCHF's work, as well as the role they played in the creation of our art. We are not affiliated with Nike, as we have intentionally chosen to disassociate the artist from the brand through various means. MSCHF strongly believes in the freedom of expression. Nothing is more important than our ability, and that of other artists, to continue expressing ideas. We look forward to working with Nike and the court to resolve this issue in the most expeditious manner possible. Given the current circumstances, this letter by Nike will also promote Lil Nas X's Twitter presence; the Satan Shoes Edition and the numerous accounts promoting it continues to collect unabated support during their process."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.290

Note the closing move: pointing out that Nike's lawsuit is free advertising for Lil Nas X. The statement itself is a press release disguised as a legal filing.

---

## The Settlement + The One Refund

Nike and MSCHF settled before the case reached full trial. Nike's requirement: MSCHF must fully refund any purchaser who chose to return their shoes.

**Result:**
> "Only one person opted for a refund."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.292

Out of thousands who posted outraged content on social media, **one** person actually wanted their money back. Satan Shoes traded on StockX and eBay at **4× retail price** during the lawsuit period.

This is documented proof that online moral outrage and actual purchasing behavior are almost completely decoupled. The controversy was the marketing. The product was validated by the market in real time.

**Final agreement:** MSCHF sent Nike three pairs of Jesus Shoes + three pairs of Satan Shoes — "for their own personal use." Nike now owns the diptych.
