# Severed Spots

**Archive #:** 24
**Year:** 2020
**Format:** Participatory destruction / edition
**Source:** *Made by MSCHF* Book p.44, p.147–150 + Amy Adler essay + Cattelan/Papini essay
**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## One-Line Concept

Bought a Damien Hirst spot painting, cut it into 88 individual spots, sold each separately.

---

## Full Description

MSCHF purchased a Damien Hirst spot print — a canvas of 88 colored dots, each a distinct color against white. They cut the canvas into 88 individual spots and sold each piece separately at $480 each. A second edition used a different Hirst print (*Fluniprene*, 2008), producing 100 spots at $1,500–$2,500 each in collaboration with Perrotin Gallery New York.

## Origin: COVID Pivot

**Severed Spots was born from COVID-19 constraints — not pre-planned.**

> "When COVID-19 hit New York City in March 2020 and all nonessential business employees were instructed to stay home, MSCHF employees continued to work on their two-week drop schedule. However, we had to pivot to prioritize works feasible under the pandemic conditions. Physical works were more challenging. We looked at the possibility of selling a finished product and using it as raw material for new drops. Simultaneously, COVID-19 timely provoked us to use artistic precedent of rallying people to buy art as a collective community gesture."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.149

**The pandemic context made the drop's logic more powerful:** In a time of collective crisis, buying a fragment of a destroyed artwork was a communal act — 88 people each hold a piece of the same destroyed thing.

## Execution

- Cutting was done in a controlled, clinical manner — team wore white lab coats and clean-room gloves
- The methodical precision signals laboratory procedure AND art-handling protocol simultaneously
- MSCHF carved their name into the pipe fittings of the Met Sink (the same clinical precision applied here)

## Legal Analysis

Amy Adler (NYU Art Law) analyzed Severed Spots in the book's "Against the Law" essay:

**Moral rights violation:** Hirst's moral rights protect his work from destruction. But MSCHF argues destruction IS the art — placing them in the tradition of Duchamp's reciprocal readymade.

> "MSCHF placed itself in a long tradition of artists who use destruction as a critical tool."
> — Amy Adler, *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.44

**Parallels cited:**
- Rauschenberg's *Erased de Kooning Drawing* (1953) — using an art tool as creative gesture
- Chapman Brothers' mutilation of rare Goya prints

**Legal outcome:** No lawsuit resulted. The art/destruction argument held.

---

## Financial Logic

- Hirst spot prints sell at auction for significant sums — the connected canvas has a higher value than any individual spot
- By severing the spots: total value of 88 × $480 = $42,240 (first edition)
- Second edition: 100 × $1,500–$2,500 = $150,000–$250,000
- The act of destruction created value — 88 owners now each hold a unique piece of a destroyed Hirst

## Companion Product: 88 Holes

The destruction produced TWO products, not one.

- **88 Spots** — the 88 individual cut spots sold at $480 each (online)
- **88 Holes** — the original canvas with 88 holes cut out, sold as a separate object

Total products from one destroyed Hirst: 89 (88 spots + 1 holed canvas).

**Source:** *Book p.152* [HIGH confidence]

---

## Buyer Package (What Each Spot Came With)

Each $480 spot shipped with:
1. The individual cut spot (framed or unframed)
2. **USB drive** containing a time-lapse video of the full cutting process
3. **Postcard** marking the purchased spot's exact location on the original print

The documentation of the destruction was part of the product. Each buyer holds evidence of the act, not just the result.

**Source:** *Book p.151* [HIGH confidence]

## MSCHF Gallery Formal Entity

The Severed Spots project confirmed MSCHF operates a formal gallery entity with an official seal: **"MSCHF GALLERY / NEW YORK, NY / AUTHENTIC"** — an embossed stamp used to certify works.

This entity was used to enter Art Basel Miami (ATM Leaderboard, 2022) and to certify editions like Severed Spots.

**Source:** *Book p.153* [HIGH confidence]

## One Drop Seeds the Next

**Second edition correction:** Source print is *Fluniprene* (2007), not 2008. 108 spots (not 100). The holed canvas was sandwiched between photos and hung as an installation at Perrotin.

**2022 extension — oil paintings:** MSCHF commissioned a series of oil paintings reproducing individual AI-generated images from *This Foot Does Not Exist* at their first Perrotin show (*No More Tears, I'm Lovin' It*, 2022). This is the documented MSCHF pattern: digital/conceptual drop → physical art object years later.

Severed Spots itself followed this pattern: the Hirst destruction (2020) → gallery installation of 108 individually framed spots (Perrotin NY, 2022) → the holed canvas continues circulating in galleries.

**Source:** *Book p.154–159* [HIGH confidence]
