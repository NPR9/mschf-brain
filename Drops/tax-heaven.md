## One-Line Concept

Free TurboTax competitor in the form of an anime dating simulator — you date "Iris" (named after the IRS) and she files your taxes by asking increasingly personal questions.

---

## Full Description

Tax Heaven 3000 (TH3K) is a free visual novel video game that generates a fileable 2022 US federal income tax return. The player goes on dates with Iris — an anime character — in a Japanese café. As the relationship deepens, her questions become increasingly personal: what do you do for work? Are you single? Do you have dependents? What's your Social Security number? Complete the game, file your taxes.

The game is **genuinely functional** — it processes a real Form 1040. It was **genuinely free** — no subscription, no upsell, no dark patterns.

## The Target: TurboTax's FUD Doctrine

MSCHF identified TurboTax's internal design principle as the explicit target:

> "TurboTax parasitically extracts profit from a bottleneck built around a public process and deploys a laundry list of corporate scam practices."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.192

TurboTax's internal documents reference a guiding principle: **FUD — Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt.** The product deliberately confuses users into paying for services they don't need. TH3K generates the inverse: **Pink UX** — trust, intimacy, and play.

> "TH3K endeavors to generate the opposite of FUD by the nature of its dating sim format."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.195

**Additional target:** TurboTax lobbied Congress for years to prevent the IRS from offering free direct filing. The IRS eventually built "Direct File" — its own free tool with a similar questionnaire structure to TH3K.

## Development Notes

- 4th MSCHF video game
- Built on Ren'Py (open-source visual novel engine)
- Reference game: *Doki Doki Literature Club* (2017) — a dating sim with horror elements emerging through gameplay
- TH3K's horror equivalent: completing the game and discovering you've filed your actual taxes
- Development required extreme familiarity with US tax code to ensure accuracy
- The game's questionnaire exactly mirrors Form 1040 pacing — each "date" corresponds to a tax form section

## Dark UX vs Pink UX

| TurboTax (Dark UX) | TH3K (Pink UX) |
|--------------------|----------------|
| FUD: Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt | Trust, intimacy, play |
| Loading bars to suggest complexity | Anime expressions to suggest care |
| Upsells disguised as requirements | Genuinely free, no upsells |
| Opacity about what "free" means | Transparency through character dialogue |
| Animated characters saying "Hmm" | Iris saying "Hmm" — but sincerely |

TurboTax uses fake animated characters to create false intimacy. TH3K uses a fake animated character to create genuine utility. The mechanisms are identical; the intent is opposite.
