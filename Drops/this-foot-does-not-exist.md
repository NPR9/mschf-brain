# This Foot Does Not Exist (TFDNE)

**Archive #:** 12
**Year:** 2019
**Format:** SMS bot / AI image generator
**Source:** *Made by MSCHF* Book p.160–172 (full Absurd Systems documentation)
**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## One-Line Concept

Trained a GAN on foot images, connected it to an SMS number — text the bot, receive AI-generated feet.

---

## Full Description

In 2019 MSCHF trained a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) to produce foot images and connected it to an SMS bot at 607-405-3339. A user texted the number to request feet; they received between one and ten AI-generated foot pictures in response. The bot sent a follow-up asking if the user was satisfied; if not, more feet were available on request.

The website: **ThisFootDoesNotExist.com**

---

## Why SMS — Medium Chosen to Match Cultural Behavior

The SMS format was not chosen for convenience. It was chosen because it replicates the actual mechanics of the foot pic economy:

> "Asking for and receiving feet pics is a deliberately clunky interaction, similar to the feeling of buying drugs from a stranger: you're not sure if they're clean. And yet the text message exchange created an intimacy — akin to sexting with a new lover."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.162

The clunkiness IS the experience. A frictionless app would have killed the concept. SMS maintained the social discomfort, privacy, and intimacy of real foot pic transactions — while replacing the human seller with a GAN.

**Principle at work:** The medium was chosen to match the cultural behavior of the system being critiqued, not for user convenience. The UX is the critique.

---

## The Foot Pic Economy (Context)

MSCHF researched the foot pic micromarket extensively before building TFDNE:

- Foot pics are one of the most liquid, lowest-risk ways to monetize fetish content
- The economy predates OnlyFans and normalized peer-to-peer fetish transactions
- Gender imbalance: producers predominantly women; buyers statistically men
- A Business Insider article documented one seller earning $115,000/year
- The market is low-stakes, relatively anonymous, and operates via direct message

TFDNE replaced the human seller with a GAN — producing infinite supply at zero marginal cost — while maintaining the exact same social/UX format.

---

## Technical Architecture (2019)

- Required **two separate GANs**: one to process/learn from training data, one to generate output
- Training dataset: thousands of foot images scraped/compiled by MSCHF
- Output: realistic but imperfect — subtle artifacts (extra toes, warped geometry, uncanny proportions)
- As of 2024: a single diffusion model could replace both GANs entirely

The GAN artifacts — the imperfect, slightly-wrong-toed feet — became content in their own right when the 2022 oil painting series was made.

---

## UX Journey (Four States)

MSCHF designed a four-state user experience:
1. **What is a Foot?** — Cultural context layer
2. **Foot pics as Commodified by Social Content** — Market context
3. **Foot pics as Unboxed Substance** — The transaction
4. **Return to Question** — Post-transaction reflection

The UX was a critique of the economy it replicated, not just a prank.

---

## One Drop Seeds the Next: 2022 Oil Paintings

Three years after TFDNE launched, MSCHF made a series of **oil paintings** based on the AI-generated feet:

- *This Foot Does Not Exist 1–8*, 2022
- GAN-generated image, oil on canvas
- 70¾ × 50¾ in. (179.8 × 129 cm) each
- Debuted at Perrotin New York (*No More Tears, I'm Lovin' It*, 2022)

The canvases highlight the **painterly artifacting** present in image generation — the AI's imperfect toes, warped proportions, and digital distortions become visible as formal painterly qualities when rendered in oil. As commercial AI became photorealistic, the *imperfect* feet became the content.

> "As commercial AI has become fully photorealistic, the distinct aesthetic of imperfect feet — the evident marks of our tool — have become a part of the content of the work."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.171

**The MSCHF pattern documented:** Digital/conceptual drop (2019) → physical fine art object (2022). One drop seeds the next.

## Cultural Commodification Reference

Cited in book: TFDNE directly anticipated the rise of OnlyFans (launched 2016, exploded 2020–2021). The foot pic economy TFDNE modeled became mainstream pop culture within two years of the drop. MSCHF was operating inside a micromarket before it became a macro trend.
