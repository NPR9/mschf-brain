## 23. Always Punch Something, Never Punch Down

**Definition:** Every MSCHF drop has a target. The target must be a system, institution, corporation, or power structure — never an individual, subculture, or vulnerable group. Punching up is required; punching sideways or down is disqualifying.

**In their own words:**
> "The work has to punch up, not down. We're always asking: who is this making fun of? If the answer is anyone who isn't in power, we stop."
> — MSCHF (paraphrased from multiple transcripts)

**Confidence:** HIGH [Appears in Book Handbook section; applies consistently across all documented drops]

**The test applied to every drop:**
- TurboTax (Tax Heaven 3000): monopolistic corporation extracting profit from a public process → legitimate target
- Nike (Jesus/Satan Shoes): one of the world's most powerful brand conglomerates → legitimate target
- Damien Hirst (Severed Spots): one of the wealthiest artists alive → legitimate target
- Boston Dynamics (Spot's Rampage): defense contractor hiding military applications behind cute PR → legitimate target
- ATM Leaderboard: the ultra-wealthy at Art Basel → legitimate target
- Lil Nas X fans (Satan Shoes): NOT a target — the work aligned with them, not against them

**What it rules out:** Drops that mock marginalized groups, subcultures, or individuals without power. MSCHF's humor comes from exposing absurdity in systems, not from punching at people.
