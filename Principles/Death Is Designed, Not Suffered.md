## 21. Death Is Designed, Not Suffered

**Definition:** How a project ends is as creatively considered as how it launches. MSCHF designs its own archival preservation and ends projects on its own terms. There is an internal plan to "burn it all down." Endings are content.

**In their own words (from Handbook footnotes):**
> "Projects end! How things die is part of the content of the work."
> — MSCHF Handbook (internal), *Book p.15, fn.1*

> "MSCHF designs its own archival preservation and ends projects on its own terms."
> — MSCHF Handbook (internal), *Book p.15, fn.2*

> "MSCHF has a plan to burn it all down."
> — MSCHF Handbook (internal), *Book p.15, fn.4*

**Confidence:** HIGH [Book p.15]

**Visual:** Handbook showed LEGO-style funeral scene and rebirth/blooming scene — death and birth treated as equivalent creative moments.

**Why it matters:** Most brands dread product death. MSCHF scripts it. The "death strategy" (transcript: "get excited before it's real → it's real → it dies") is a handbook rule, not a tactical decision.

**Proves it:** [[Key4All]] (car died when engine burned out — the death was part of the arc), [[Big Red Boots]] (dropped at peak — deliberate exit before decline)

**Related:** [[Distribution Is a Design Discipline]] (Principle 4) — the death strategy is the third moment in the 3-moment distro framework.
