## 24. Make Objects With a Point of View

**Definition:** Every physical MSCHF object should express a specific opinion or argument — not just be aesthetically interesting. The object IS the argument. Form follows concept, not taste.

**Confidence:** MEDIUM [Referenced in drop pages; implied across the full catalog but not explicitly named as a handbook principle]

**In practice:** The MSCHF Spotted Fluted Vase (#96) has a documented point of view about decorative objects and their relationship to cultural utility. Big Red Boots argue for the platonic ideal of form. Microscopic Handbags argue that luxury is purely symbolic. Every object has a thesis.

**Distinction from Cultural Ready-Made:** Cultural Ready-Made is about borrowing existing meaning from cultural material. This principle is about what MSCHF *does* with that material — it must express an opinion, not just comment or describe.

**Proves it:** [[Drops/microscopic-handbags]] (thesis: luxury value is entirely symbolic, zero material required), [[Drops/infinite-relic]] (thesis: the relic system's authenticity claims are biologically exploitable)
