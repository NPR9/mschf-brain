## 9. One Sentence + Three Sentence Rule

**Definition:** A good idea must land in one sentence (the hook) AND hit harder in three (deeper layers). Multiple comprehension layers = multiple audiences = inherently pressworthy.

**In their own words:**
> "A good idea has to hit in one sentence and hit even harder in three sentences. What that means is there are layers of comprehension. And typically those layers means it applies to multiple audiences."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

> "A good idea should slap in one sentence and then slap even harder in three. You appreciate it for the headline but if you really dig into it you're like — wait, is this thing making fun of me? And do I still like it? And if I buy it am I a chump or am I a patron of the arts?"
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*

**Confidence:** HIGH

**Example:** [[Jesus Shoes]] — Layer 1: cool Nike collab. Layer 2: religious iconography. Layer 3: First Amendment commentary. Layer 4: am I in on the joke or the butt of it?
