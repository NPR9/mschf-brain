## 20. Real and Fake Are Made in the Same Factory

**Definition:** The distinction between authentic and counterfeit is a brand myth, not a manufacturing reality. Counterfeits are arguably more honest about the relationship between brand and product — they strip away the fiction of authenticity.

**In their own words (from Handbook footnotes):**
> "Counterfeits are not less real — they are more honest about the relationship between brand and product."
> — MSCHF Handbook (internal), *Book p.22*

**Confidence:** HIGH [Book p.22]

**Visual:** Handbook page showed Under Armour logo alongside "UNCLE MARTIAN" (Chinese knockoff brand) — presented as equivalents, not hierarchy.

**Why it matters:** This explains MSCHF's philosophical comfort when Big Red Boots fakes flooded the market. Within their own doctrine, the fakes were MORE honest than the originals — they stripped away the brand premium to expose the object.

**Also explains:** GSCT Handbag (5 factories playing telephone — which version is "real"?), Possibly Real Fairies (999 forgeries shuffled with the original — which is "real"?)

**Proves it:** [[Big Red Boots]] (fakes = philosophically consistent), [[Possibly Real Fairies]] (designed so the real cannot be identified), [[GSCT Handbag]] (reality degrades across supply chain)
