## 8. Test of Time (Idea Vetting)

**Definition:** Good ideas survive months of being ignored. MSCHF throws ideas into an ice box; if still exciting 2–6 months later, they move to production (12 months out). Reactive ideas always fail this test.

**In their own words:**
> "If we are not still interested in something two months down the line it was not a good enough idea. Thank God we didn't start working on it sooner."
> — Kevin, *_19 Mar 2024 BUSINESS OF HYPE*

> "We forced the idea to sit in that pool for another couple of months. Now it has to survive the test of time. You forget about it, and you come back to it. And if it still feels exciting, then you slot it for production, which now on our timetables could be 12 months out."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

> "Ideas have to sit in the pool for months at a time and every time we revisit the pool to add new ideas you have to kill old ones."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**Confidence:** HIGH

**Canonical 3-year proof:**
ATM Leaderboard (Archive #140) was conceived in 2019. MSCHF cold-called Art Basel's admin line and was rejected. The idea sat untouched until 2022 when gallery representation (Perrotin) enabled Art Basel access. **3 years elapsed.** The concept executed exactly as originally conceived — nothing was adapted or compromised. The wait was the proof. Idea required a specific location; MSCHF waited for the right conditions rather than finding a lesser venue.

> "MSCHF's first internal records of ATM Leaderboard date to 2019, and from the outset, the piece was conceived as an installation for Art Basel Miami Beach, which we identified as the best (perhaps only) place to install the work." — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.299
