# MSCHF Timeline: 2014–2026

> Chronological evolution. Key moments, philosophy shifts, structural changes.
> Extracted from 12 transcripts (2019–2025). Confidence noted per entry.

---

## Pre-MSCHF: Gabe's Origin (2012–2016)

**2012:** Gabe discovers Instagram after Facebook acquires it. Starts paying attention to the NYC creative landscape.

**~2013:** First project — a website offering intentionally bad Twitter advice for $1/tweet. Makes $200. Builds a viral loop accidentally (users had to tweet at him to engage, advertising his service to their followers). Gets picked up by New York Mag, Mashable, The Observer.
> "I made something from nothing... you write the code and then it exists and then the next day people are talking about it sharing it and then paying me money."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**2013:** Lands internship at BuzzFeed via cold DM. Wears an ill-fitting suit to the interview. Gets the job. Secretly starts building "Miscellaneous MSCHF" projects while at BuzzFeed — watches his own colleagues race to write about them.
> "I was putting out projects under Mischief that I would then watch people in the room race to be the first to write about and they had no idea it was me. That was the best feeling."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**2013–2019:** "Chasing the feeling" era. Multiple viral hits. Trying to understand if there's a formula. Writing internal papers since 2017 trying to codify it.
> "Every time I had a hit I was like oh my God it's kind of repeatable... by like the fifth time I was like there has got to be a way to make money doing this."
> — Gabe, *30 MAY 2024 ART OF GOING VIRAL*

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## 2016: First Client, First Test

**Casper mattress** becomes founding client. Late Night Snack Packs (interactive video site simulating going-out scenes). Insomnia Bot (SMS chatbot, 11pm–5am only). Both become viral hits.
> "Casper had reached out and that was sort of the impetus... the next test was okay they paid for it, now we need to create it and make sure it works... Late Night Snack Packs was a huge hit and that was the first thing that we ever put out."
> — Gabe, *_16 Jan 2019 GoDaddy*

**Key realization:** Brands will pay for earned media if you can prove it works. But they still want safety over novelty.

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## 2017–2018: The Persistence of Chaos Era

**Times New Roman (Bigger Is Better)** font launches. Gets on Good Morning America.

**Persistence of Chaos** — virus laptop. Sells at auction for $1.3M (~2018).

**Away Mode** (Alexa skill).

**Internal pain:** Company is growing, siloing by function (sales / creative / production). Ideas getting worse.

**Gabe's honeymoon awakening:** Returns from honeymoon and launches "Mischief Madness" — 4-day workweek, Fridays = brainstorm hackathon, no laptops. Saves the company.
> "It kept me from shutting down the company last year."
> — Gabe, *_24 Feb 2020 Hacking Culture*

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## 2019: Going Formal + Jesus Shoes

**MSCHF formally founded** as a company. Raises VC money from Silicon Valley with pitch of "holding company for creative enterprises."

**Kevin and Lucas join / formalize** (exact date unclear — they describe working together "since the day we met" at RISD before MSCHF, and joining the founding group).

**Jesus Shoes** — first major physical drop. Nike Air Max 97 + holy water. Goes viral across multiple audiences simultaneously.
> "We sold a lot more than 12 of them. But the funny thing coming out from that was watching the different layers of appreciation."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**New Museum's New Inc program:** Kevin and Lucas were part of this art/tech incubator post-RISD. Important formative environment.

**Philosophy (2019):** MSCHF = "we create internet for the internet." Primarily digital. Brands pay for earned impressions. Every two weeks, something new.

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## 2020–2021: Pandemic Acceleration

**COVID hits.** MSCHF thrives. Remote-compatible format. Digital drops continue.

**Satan Shoes** (Lil Nas X). Nike lawsuit. First major legal battle — MSCHF wins on artistic expression grounds. Establishes legal strategy: in court, MSCHF is always an art collective.

**Possibly Real Fairies by Andy Warhol by MSCHF** — 999 Warhol forgeries.

**Helpful VCs** — crypto punk project for charity.

**MSCHF Socks Pallet** — Costco-style monopoly experiment. Florida Sock Queen emerges.

**Key shift:** Physical objects start outperforming digital. Media landscape has shifted — influencers and creators replace media companies as primary distributors. Physical objects that are "amazing in person but also jump off a screen" become the dominant format.
> "That opened up this whole Pandora's box of where you can go with physical objects."
> — Gabe, *15 Oct 2025 outlasting hype*

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## 2022: Art World Entry + Key4All + ATM Leaderboard

**Perrotin Gallery relationship begins.** Cold-called trying to get ATM machine into Art Basel. 30-minute Zoom with Emanuel Perrotin becomes 2 hours. Leads to gallery representation + show.
> "I did not know about galleries in 2022... all I knew was they gave me a list of galleries that had booths at Art Basel and I started calling them."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Key4All** drops. PT Cruiser + 5,000 keys. Car travels coast to coast for 9 months before engine burns out. Community forms. Car ends up in LA gallery.

**ATM Leaderboard** at Art Basel Miami. Massive crowd. Sold for $75K. Diplo checks in at $3M. Person with $37 becomes crowd hero.

**MSCHF becomes legible to the fine art world.** Represented by a major gallery. Works in museums.

**Also 2022:** Tax Heaven 3000 (released early 2023 for 2022 tax year).

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## 2023: Big Red Boots + Peak Virality + Korean Retrospective

**Big Red Boots** drop (spring 2023). Originally ~200–300 pairs at $350. Single Instagram mirror pic goes viral. Algorithm + organic sharing simultaneously. 100,000+ orders within an hour. Black market fakes flood within weeks. Meta uses it as case study for digital marketers. Still their most culturally known drop.

**Micro Handbag** — microscopic, sells at $63,750 at Paris Fashion Week auction.

**GSCT Handbag** — telephone game across 5 factories. So successful it becomes its own sub-brand.

**Met Sink** — Ship of Theseus applied to the Metropolitan Museum. Piece technically in the Met bathroom.

**Korean Museum Retrospective** (November 2023). Breaks attendance records. K-pop stars, Squid Game cast. Gabe reconnects with Korean cultural identity.
> "There is actually like a shared international language of creativity... all those kids had so much in common actually with the kids that show up at our New York opening."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Post-Big-Red-Boots realization:** Went too close to the sun. Fakes flooded the market. MSCHF calls factories, ramps production, still can't compete with Shein. Active decision: don't be the Big Red Boot company. Don't be a shoe company.
> "We never wanted to be a big red boot company. We don't even want to be a shoe company."
> — Gabe, *9 Oct 2024 making MSCHF*

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## 2024: Structural Maturity + Existential Questioning

**Paintball Robot Dog** (Boston Dynamics Spot). Boston Dynamics objects.

**Tax Heaven 3000** gets full physical release (CD packaging, Steam). #1 on Steam for 1–2 weeks.

**MSCHF restructures as holding company.** Each category (footwear, luxury leather goods, fine art, other TBD) gets own sub-brand, team, budget, P&L.

**Growing pains:** Becoming too big = becoming legible = losing edge. Active tension.
> "By law, the less cool you become. It is just the way things work, which is very painful."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Virality feels different.** After enough viral hits, the feeling fades. "You feel nothing."
> "There was a time when we would go viral and it felt amazing. Then, after enough times, you feel nothing."
> — Gabe, *15 Oct 2025 outlasting hype*

**Existential questioning:** Where does the excitement actually come from? Need to rediscover the source.

**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## 2025: Applied Mischief + Long-Game Thinking

**Applied Mischief launches** — internal creative back-office opens as external client agency. MSCHF's viral-loop design capability available to brands. Not a service — a joint-venture/cultural-material approach.
> "We might as well open up that to external clients. It can become a profit center, which is great. But the other part is like maybe it can make our world a little bit bigger too, because we're hungry for more formats."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*

**Long-game thinking activated:** Seriously discussing disappearing for 20 years and coming back. 50-year stunts. Legacy.
> "What if we disappear for 20 years and then come back? That would actually be the coolest thing we could do."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*

**AI response:** Not rushing in. Same logic as crypto — when everyone's running toward the cliff, wait.
> "Unlike the whole crypto craziness of the last couple of years, we didn't get into that because we didn't feel like it was here to stay yet... There's a little bit of a similar sentiment right now [with AI]."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 -Gabe Whaley on Why MSCHF Makes Virality an Art Form*

**White space opportunity identified:** As AI floods content, scarcity shifts to craftsmanship, real-world permanence, physical goods, secrets held over time.
> "The meta is how do you build permanence? How do you build things in the real world? How do you elevate craftsmanship? And how do you have the discipline to create and maintain secrets over a long period of time?"
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**10-year vision:** Show up in art school textbooks. Horizontally stacked holding company of creative enterprises. The 2019 VC pitch — realized.
> "If we're still around in 10 years, maybe we start to show up in some of the history books, possibly."
> — Gabe, *oct 2025 Making MSCHF, an Art and Discipline*

**Confidence:** HIGH
