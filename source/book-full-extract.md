## PAGES 6–29: MSCHF HANDBOOK

> Context (from p.7): In 2019 MSCHF made an internal handbook, inspired by art movement manifestos and the instructional nature of the KLF's "The Manual." Comprised precepts and frameworks for the first years of existence. The handbook was entirely full-bleed images and single declarative sentences. Presented here with annotations — MSCHF's thoughts on working tenets at the time they were created. Some have expired. Some are in some ways contained within this book.

### PAGE 130 — KEY4ALL: Description + Genesis
**Page type:** Text-heavy
**Sections:** DESCRIPTION / GENESIS
**Description:** MSCHF bought a 2004 Chrysler PT Cruiser and made five thousand keys. Keyholders could turn it on and drive it. If anyone left the car unlocked, it could be discovered and driven by another keyholder. Keyholders were provided a phone number connected to the car's GPS.
**Genesis:** "We planned to make one thousand keys to a Manhattan apartment block, allowing anyone to claim a key and search for the car from a central location. However, imagining the scenario in which a thousand apartment hunters all followed their instincts at the same time quickly led us to discard this idea. We wanted to avoid any mishaps, especially our participants being mistaken for intruders. Therefore we raised the number of concurrent to five thousand."
**Key insight on scale decision:** "We set up the public phone number that reported the car's location and speed. The car also had multiple GPS AirTags. We also integrated a Discord server, providing additional information to keyholders."
**Photo:** Drivers pose with the original, unmodified PT Cruiser.
**NEW KNOWLEDGE:** The original plan was apartment keys — not a car. The shift to 5,000 (not 1,000) was a deliberate scale increase to avoid mistaken-for-intruder situations. Discord was central infrastructure from launch.

### PAGE 131 — KEY4ALL: Competition vs. Communalism
**Page type:** Text-heavy
**Section:** COMPETITION VS. COMMUNALISM
**Core argument:** "Pivoting the project from an apartment to a car shifted the focus from private use to communalism. **Key insight:** "However, imagining the scenario in which a thousand apartment hunters all followed their instincts simultaneously would have reduced interaction to a competitive scramble, and there was no solution to this problem. In the object of desire, the car, became communal."

### PAGE 300
**Type:** Text (Development, Reverse Engineering)
**Section:** Absurd Objects — ATM Leaderboard

**DEVELOPMENT + REVERSE ENGINEERING:**
- "The development of ATM Leaderboard was fundamentally a process of reverse engineering. MSCHF's first prototype was stymied when our ATM would not display balances that hit seven digits."

- Solution: walked to every ATM within half-mile radius of Brooklyn studio, attempted to check balance on a card connected to a one-million-dollar bank account

### PAGE 360 (Virality vs. Success — CRITICAL STATEMENT)

**VIRALITY VS. SUCCESS:**

> "There is a common misconception about MSCHF that by-the-numbers virality is synonymous with success. In our mind, the Big Red Boot is far from MSCHF's most successful release. As ubiquity became the boots' defining feature, the audience lost the ability to actively opt in to the fictitious abstractions we intended to elevate as an object developed into memetic noise."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.360

> "Yet there is still something compelling about the boots. They were integrated into the fabric of online existence in a way that few objects ever are, with the crowd actively taking ownership of their image. This is the kind of dynamic we love. Watching the effective collapse of the Big Red Boot distinction between fashion object and viral image is perhaps the most instructive to the general paradigm virtuous of MSCHF's other works; the project becomes a soup or structure that is ultimately played out by its audience."
> — *Made by MSCHF*, Book p.360

**The key distinction MSCHF draws:**
- **Virality** = crowd takes ownership of image; memetic replication; concept dissolves into noise
- **Success** = audience actively interrogates the themes; the confusion remains generative
- BRB achieved the first but lost the second at scale
- "The boots became the default image of MSCHF" — this is described as a PROBLEM, not a triumph
